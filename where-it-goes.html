What Happens to Your Disbursement

Where the money goes and how to track it

The Money Never Touches Your Hands First

When your loan is disbursed, it doesn't go to you. It goes directly to your school.

Your school then applies it to your account in a specific order.

The Payment Hierarchy

Your school is required to use your loan money in this order:

1. Tuition and Fees (First Priority)

  • Tuition for the semester
  • Mandatory fees (technology, health, activity, etc.)
  • These are automatically deducted

2. School-Billed Charges (Second Priority)

  • On-campus housing
  • Meal plans
  • Other school-provided services you've signed up for

3. Refund to You (What's Left Over)

If there's money remaining after covering your school charges, you get a refund.

Important

The refund is not "extra money." It's loan money you borrowed. You'll have to pay it back with interest.

Use it for legitimate education expenses: books, supplies, off-campus rent, transportation, food.

How Refunds Work

Timing

Schools must issue refunds within 14 days of disbursement or the first day of classes, whichever is later.

Delivery Methods

  • Direct deposit – Fastest option (2-3 business days)
  • Paper check – Slower (7-10 business days)
  • School debit card – Some schools offer this option

Set up direct deposit in your student portal if you haven't already. It's significantly faster.

What If You Borrowed Too Much?

If you realize you don't need the full loan amount, you can return all or part of it within 120 days of disbursement without paying interest.

How to Return Loan Money

  1. Contact your school's financial aid office
  2. Tell them you want to return part or all of your loan
  3. They'll process the return and notify your loan servicer

This is especially useful if you got a refund and realize you don't need it for education expenses.

Tracking Your Disbursement

In Your Student Portal

Your school's student portal will show:

  • Expected disbursement dates
  • Disbursement amounts
  • How the money was applied to your account
  • Refund status

On the National Student Loan Data System (NSLDS)

Visit StudentAid.gov to see:

  • All your federal loans
  • Disbursement history
  • Current balances
  • Loan servicer information

From Your Loan Servicer

Your loan servicer (the company that manages your loan) will send you:

  • Disbursement notices
  • Account statements
  • Current balance updates

Common Issues and How to Fix Them

Disbursement Didn't Happen

Check for holds: Log into your student portal and look for any blocks or to-do items.

Verify enrollment: Make sure you're enrolled at least half-time.

Contact financial aid: If everything looks clear, call your financial aid office.

Refund Is Less Than Expected

Review your account charges: Your school may have added fees or charges you weren't expecting.

Check for outstanding balances: Some schools apply current disbursements to previous balances.

Refund Hasn't Arrived

Check the 14-day window: Schools have up to 14 days to issue refunds.

Verify your direct deposit info: Wrong account info will delay your refund.

Contact the bursar's office: They handle refund processing.
