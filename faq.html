Loan Disbursement FAQ

Hey, it's Marcus. Howard University senior, financial aid peer advisor, first-gen student from Atlanta. I help students navigate this stuff every day. Here are the questions I get asked all the time.

Questions Students Ask Me All The Time

I work as a financial aid peer advisor at Howard. These are the questions I get asked every single day. Let me answer them for you.

When will my student loan money actually show up?

For most students, your loan disburses within 10 days before or after classes start. Your school picks the exact date.

But if this is your FIRST federal loan ever, add 30 days to that. So if classes start August 20, you're looking at late September.

Check your student portal for the exact date. Don't guess—know.

Why is there a hold on my disbursement?

Okay, so this happens a lot. Here are the usual suspects:

  • Entrance counseling not done – Takes 30 minutes online, do it now
  • Master Promissory Note (MPN) not signed – This is the legal contract, you have to sign it
  • Enrollment issues – You might not be enrolled in enough credits (need at least 6 for half-time)
  • Missing documents – Check your financial aid to-do list
  • First-time borrower delay – That 30-day wait I keep mentioning

Go to your student portal right now and check for holds. Clear them today, not tomorrow.

Do I get all my loan money at once?

No. Never.

If you borrowed for the full year, you get half in fall and half in spring. That's how it works for everyone.

You can't change this. You can't ask for it all upfront. It's federal law.

What's this 30-day delay everyone keeps talking about?

If you've never taken out a federal student loan before, the government makes you wait 30 days after classes start before your first disbursement.

They say it's to give you time to reconsider. In reality, it means first-gen students like me are scrambling to cover costs for a month.

This only happens once—your very first loan. After that, you're on the normal schedule.

Plan for this gap. Ask family, look for emergency loans from your school, work extra hours before school starts. Don't get caught off guard like I did.

Where does my loan money go when it's disbursed?

It goes to your school first. Not to you.

Your school uses it to pay (in this order):

  1. Tuition
  2. Mandatory fees
  3. On-campus housing and meal plan (if you have them)

Whatever's left over comes to you as a refund. That's when you can use it for books, rent, food, etc.

How long until I get my refund?

After your loan disburses to your school, they have up to 14 days to send you the refund.

If you set up direct deposit, it'll hit your account 2-3 business days after they process it.

If you're waiting for a paper check, add another week for mail time.

Set up direct deposit if you haven't already. It's way faster.

Can I cancel my loan after it's been disbursed?

Yes! You have 120 days from disbursement to return all or part of your loan without paying any interest.

Contact your financial aid office and tell them you want to return it. They'll process it.

This is smart if you got a refund and realize you don't actually need it. Don't keep loan money you don't need—you'll pay interest on it for years.

What happens if I drop classes?

If you drop below half-time (usually 6 credits) BEFORE your loan disburses, the disbursement gets canceled.

If you drop AFTER it disburses, you might have to pay some or all of it back. And you'll enter your grace period, which starts the clock on repayment.

Don't drop classes without talking to financial aid first. Seriously. The consequences are real.

My disbursement date passed but I don't have my money. What do I do?

First, check your student portal for holds. Clear anything that's pending.

Second, make sure you're enrolled in enough credits.

Third, if everything looks good, call financial aid. Don't email—call. You need answers now, not in 3-5 business days.

Can I speed up my disbursement?

No. The timeline is set by federal law and your school's policies.

What you CAN do is make sure you're not the reason for a delay:

  • Complete all paperwork as soon as it's available
  • Clear any holds immediately
  • Make sure you're enrolled in enough credits
  • Set up direct deposit for faster refunds
