Loan Disbursement FAQ

Common Questions About Loan Disbursement

Get quick answers to the most frequently asked questions about when and how your student loan money arrives.

When will my student loan money be disbursed?

Federal student loans are typically disbursed at the beginning of each semester, usually 10 days before classes start. However, first-time borrowers may experience a 30-day delay due to federal regulations.

Why is there a hold on my disbursement?

Common reasons for disbursement holds include:

  • Incomplete entrance counseling
  • Missing Master Promissory Note (MPN)
  • Enrollment verification issues
  • Outstanding financial aid requirements
  • First-time borrower 30-day delay

Do I get all my loan money at once?

No. Student loans are disbursed in multiple installments, typically one per semester or academic term. For a full academic year, you'll usually receive two disbursements: one in fall and one in spring.

What is the 30-day disbursement delay for first-time borrowers?

Federal law requires a 30-day delay between the first day of classes and the first disbursement for first-time, first-year undergraduate borrowers. This "cooling-off period" helps ensure students understand their loan obligations.

Where does my loan money go when it's disbursed?

Loan funds are sent directly to your school's financial aid office. The school first applies the money to:

  1. Tuition
  2. Fees
  3. On-campus housing (if applicable)

Any remaining amount (refund) is then issued to you for other education expenses.

How long does it take to receive my refund after disbursement?

After your school receives the disbursement, it typically takes 3-5 business days to process and issue your refund. The refund is usually sent via direct deposit or paper check, depending on your preference.

Can I cancel my loan after it's been disbursed?

Yes! You have 120 days from the disbursement date to cancel all or part of your loan without paying interest or fees. Contact your school's financial aid office to initiate a cancellation.

What if I drop below half-time enrollment?

If you drop below half-time enrollment before your loan is disbursed, the disbursement may be canceled. If it's already been disbursed, you may need to return some or all of the funds, and you'll enter your grace period.
